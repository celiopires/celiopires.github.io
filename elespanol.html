El Español

“A news website that Spain may need to keep an eye on” by Peter Preston, The Guardian

Bio

El Español is an online daily news page launched in October 2015 after raising more than €3 million in a crowdfunding campaign. It operates from Madrid (Spain) as an independent source of news and it publishes primarily breaking news, generalist content, long in-depth articles, video coverage, picture galleries, blogs, interactive articles and interactive charts for electoral campaigns.

Led by Pedro J. Ramirez former director of El Mundo, journalist, entrepreneur and one of the most influential persons in the country. At the age of 64 years old he was laid off from the company he founded in 1989 which currently is one of the most popular news sources with roughly 100 million unique users monthly and turned all his attention to setting up a new media company El Español along with a few high-ranking editors from other news organizations including El Mundo, El País or Huffington to reveal meaningful information mainly about Spain’s political actions.

By March 2018 El Español reached the 32,5 million unique users monthly, went to the top 10 most used news sources in the country and quickly became one of the most trusted sources of information.

Design

In the past, a lot of printed Newspapers were designed by journalists to reduce expenses. While on the surface this might seem a natural way to operate, for many obvious reasons media companies became more competitive when they started to take advantages from well designed experiences and quick product deliveries if they hire designers, engineers, marketeers, and so on …

El Español, as a native media company has a lot in common with other organizations but what really makes the difference is their way of thinking. The user centered Design, the unique user experiences across devices, being mobile first or even building responsive layouts, were definitely the right keys to successfully compete against bigger organisations.

The major point about building El Español or about building products in house is, that it’s critical to build something that users will love in the future, so the design process is organised like this:

1 - Idea / briefing

It’s a document that outlines the main concept of the project, it’s functionality and the goals to be achieved

2 - Research

Crucial to define the viability of the idea and to measure the how sustainable it is

3 - Wireframing

The lo-fi prototyping first. Positioning the main idea with the elements side by side with some high level of usability, the user experience is going to shine here

4 - Ux testing

Testing it on personas and on potential users. it allows changing inefficient solutions and it helps designers to evaluate the good and the bad decisions

5 - User interface design

Interfaces exist to be used! At this stage we make sure the environment we create fits user needs

6 - Validation

This is where design and technology meet again and we make sure it all works on any device, browser and on any resolution
